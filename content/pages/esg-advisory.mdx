## Sustainability is a priority—but it’s hard to know where to start

Across India, more and more businesses want to take action on sustainability. The pressure is real—regulations are shifting, investors are watching, and global brands expect transparency. But the journey can feel overwhelming. The frameworks are technical. The expectations are high. And the biggest question often is: how do we actually do this on the ground?

At GuruJal, we help companies move from intent to action. We meet them where they are, understand their needs, and build simple, practical systems they can own and sustain. Because sustainability isn’t just about reports. It’s about people, culture, and daily decisions.

![Pearl Global case study](/uploads/2025/06/pearl-global.jpeg) **Case Study:** **Pearl Global – Leading by doing**

Pearl Global, a leading fashion manufacturer with over seven factories in India, partnered with GuruJal to take their sustainability efforts to the next level. They had a clear vision—to be net-zero and water-positive by 2045—but needed guidance on how to get there in a way that felt grounded and achievable.

We started by developing their first GRI-based sustainability report. This involved data collection across factories, utility mapping, and engaging internal teams to understand their strengths and gaps. Then, together, we built their long-term transition plan:

-   A carbon reduction roadmap aligned with SBTi principles
-   A 40% GHG reduction target by 2030
-   A 1 MW renewable energy plan
-   A water offset strategy using ETP/STP upgrades, rainwater harvesting, and source recharge
-   ESG trainings for over 10 internal teams

Within one year, they had already begun using significant amount of recycled water, improved energy efficiency per garment, and established internal dashboards to track progress. More importantly, their teams owned the process—from factory managers to top leadership. Pearl Global now leads not only through output, but through example.
